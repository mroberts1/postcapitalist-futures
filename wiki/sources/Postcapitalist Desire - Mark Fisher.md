# Postcapitalist Desire: The Final Lectures — Mark Fisher (2016–17 / 2020)

## Overview

Fisher's final course, delivered at Goldsmiths College, University of London, in the autumn/winter term of 2016–17. He died on Friday 13 January 2017, approximately four sessions into the course. The remaining sessions were reconstructed by students and collaborators — including Fisher's friends, colleagues, and intellectual influences — who continued the seminar in his memory, eventually becoming a public reading group called "Nothings Into Somethings."

The course was published posthumously as *Postcapitalist Desire: The Final Lectures* (Repeater Books, 2020), edited from recordings of the sessions Fisher delivered.

The syllabus is the closest thing to a complete map of the [[Acid Communism]] project Fisher was developing. Lectures 2–8 trace exactly the argument of the unfinished introduction: the counterculture as prefiguration, the feminist standpoint, the 1970s working class, libidinal Marxism, the refusal of work, and the violent neoliberal counter-revolution centred on Chile. Lectures 9–15 extend into territories Fisher was still exploring: post-Fordism, technofeminism, [[Accelerationism]], peer-to-peer networks, and [[Xenofeminism]].

---

## Arc of the Course

The course moves through four broad phases:

1. **What is postcapitalism?** (Lectures 1–2) — Contemporary postcapitalist thought (Srnicek/Williams, Mason) and the counterculture as historical prefiguration (Willis, Marcuse)
2. **Building the subject** (Lectures 3–6) — The transition from class consciousness to group consciousness; the feminist standpoint; the 1970s working class; libidinal Marxism; autonomia and the refusal of work
3. **How the future was cancelled** (Lectures 7–8) — Neoliberalism's violent origins in Chile; the cultural/political construction of the "middle" against the left
4. **Post-Fordism and its futures** (Lectures 9–15) — Post-Fordism; technofeminism and cyberfeminism; accelerationism; networked politics; touchscreen capture; xenofeminism

## Key Figures Introduced

Beyond Fisher's usual constellation, this course engages substantially with:
- **Srnicek & Williams** — contemporary left accelerationist postcapitalism; post-work politics
- **Jefferson Cowie** — labour history of the 1970s (already cited in the Acid Communism introduction)
- **Silvia Federici** — feminist Marxism; social reproduction; wages for housework
- **Lyotard** — libidinal economy; desire as political-economic force
- **Nick Land** — right accelerationism; machinic desire; capital as inhuman intelligence (Fisher's former CCRU colleague)
- **Sadie Plant / Laboria Cuboniks** — cyberfeminism and xenofeminism
- **Eden Medina** — Allende's *Cybersyn* project; socialist cybernetics
- **Paolo Virno** — post-Fordism; the multitude; general intellect
