# Synthetic Freedom

## Definition

A concept introduced by Srnicek and Williams in *[[Inventing the Future - Srnicek Williams|Inventing the Future]]* to name a substantive account of freedom that goes beyond capitalism's **negative freedom** (the formal absence of interference). Synthetic freedom recognises that a formal right without a material capacity is worthless. It is *constructed* — built through income, time, collective resources, and technology — rather than natural or given.

---

## Against Negative Freedom

Capitalism's preferred concept of freedom is **negative**: freedom *from* arbitrary interference by the state or other agents. This is the freedom of the "free world" against totalitarianism; the freedom to sell one's labour and choose between consumer goods. Its defining feature is *formal equality*: rich and poor are equally free not to be interfered with.

Problems:
- Negative freedom is fully compatible with mass poverty, starvation, homelessness, and unemployment. The poor are formally free but practically unfree.
- We are "formally free" not to take a job, but practically compelled to take whatever is on offer.
- We are "formally free" to run for political office, but without financial and social resources this freedom is empty.
- Our desires themselves are shaped by "pervasive advertising" — manufactured consent that negative freedom cannot account for.

---

## Three Components of Synthetic Freedom

**1. Provision of basic necessities**
Without income, time, health, and education, formal freedom is hollow. Classic social democratic goods — healthcare, housing, childcare, transport, internet access — are prerequisites for anyone to be *really* free, not just formally free. The liberal argument that market choice *enhances* this misses the actual cognitive and financial burdens of navigating markets for basic goods. Under synthetic freedom, high-quality public goods would be provided, freeing people from the labour of consumer navigation. [[Universal Basic Income]] and free time (shorter working week) are the monetary and temporal dimensions of this first component.

**2. Expansion of collective resources**
Freedom requires not just making existing options viable but expanding the range of available options. Social reasoning, language, collective knowledge, and political organisation are "cognitive scaffolding" that expands what individuals and communities can imagine and achieve. Anti-intellectualism is therefore a retrogression of freedom. The anti-institutional and anti-mediation tendencies of [[Folk Politics]] — valuing only unmediated face-to-face sociality — paradoxically *reduce* freedom by rejecting the collective mechanisms through which capacities are extended.

**3. Technological development**
Technology is "the source of our options, and options are the basis of a future that keeps us above the level of pawn." Expanding synthetic freedom requires systematically expanding technological capacities. This includes: [[Full Automation]] (freeing humans from degrading labour), cyborg augmentations, synthetic biology, technologically mediated reproduction — all experiments in extending what humans can do and be. The refusal of technology in the name of "naturalness" or "authenticity" is a refusal of freedom.

---

## Humanity as Open Project

Underlying synthetic freedom is a rejection of the idea that there is a fixed human nature or authentic human essence to be liberated. There is no pre-given humanity to be unlocked, no "unalienated" human concealed beneath capitalist alienation. Instead:

> "Alienation is a mode of enablement, and humanity is an incomplete vector of transformation."

This connects to Sadie Plant's formulation: liberation is not about freeing what women currently are but about the "re-engineering of what it can be to be a woman" — an open-ended collective experiment. **Emancipation** on this account is the maximal expansion of the capacity to act according to whatever desires people develop, over time, collectively. Universal emancipation is the insistent extension of this goal to the entirety of humanity.

This has clear resonances with [[Xenofeminism]]'s anti-naturalism and the CCRU's posthumanism.
