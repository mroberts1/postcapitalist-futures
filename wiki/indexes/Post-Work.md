## Key Questions

- Can the folk politics critique account for historical movement victories (civil rights, feminism) that used folk-political tactics?
- What are the mechanisms through which a post-work counter-hegemony would actually be built? (The book is stronger on what than on how.)
- How does the UBI demand relate to libertarian variants of basic income that use it to justify dismantling the welfare state?
- What is the relationship between Srnicek/Williams's political programme and Fisher's cultural-psychic analysis in Acid Communism?
- Is "hyperstition" a useful analytical concept or a rhetorical move that evades questions of material conditions?
