# Hauntology — Topic Index

Research into hauntology and related aesthetic modes: the eerie, the weird, Fisher's cultural theory, and the forms through which the outside, the absent, and the foreclosed future manifest in literature, film, and music.

---

## Core Concepts

- [[Hauntology]] — Derrida/Fisher; the spectral condition; foreclosed futures
- [[The Eerie]] — Fisher's primary concept; failure of absence/presence; the problem of agency
- [[The Weird]] — Fisher's companion concept; presence of that which does not belong; fascination

---

## Sources

### Books
- [[The Weird and the Eerie - Mark Fisher]] (2016) — primary theoretical text

## Cultural Objects Covered

### Film
- *Under the Skin* (Glazer, 2013) — alien-among-us eerie; denaturalizing naturalism
- *2001: A Space Odyssey* (Kubrick, 1968) — monolith as paradigm eerie object
- *The Shining* (Kubrick, 1980) — hotel as recording system; eerie Thanatos
- *Solaris* (Tarkovsky, 1972) — sentient alien ocean; mismatching intelligences
- *Stalker* (Tarkovsky, 1979) — the Zone; ethics of the eerie
- *Interstellar* (Nolan, 2014) — love as eerie agent; "They"
- *Don't Look Now* (Roeg, 1973) — fate as eerie agent; Venice as sound maze
- *Mulholland Drive* (Lynch, 2001) — weird thresholds and curtains
- *Inland Empire* (Lynch, 2006) — ontological subsidence; world-haemorrhaging

### Television
- *Quatermass and the Pit* (Kneale, BBC, 1958–59) — alien intimacy; we are the aliens
- *The Stone Tape* (Kneale, BBC, 1972) — matter as recording medium; inorganic replay
- *Quatermass* (Kneale, ITV, 1979) — eerie Thanatos; stone circles as scar tissue

### Fiction
- *Picnic at Hanging Rock* (Lindsay, 1967) — eerie as liberation; positive outside
- *The Birds* / *Don't Look Now* (du Maurier) — failure of absence; fate; collective animal agency
- *The Affirmation* / *The Glamour* (Priest) — amnesia; confabulation; negative hallucination
- *Red Shift* (Garner, 1973) — mythic time; inorganic artefact; non-linear repetition
- *The Owl Service* (Garner, 1967) — myth as fatal structure
- *Surfacing* (Atwood, 1972) — narrator as eerie gap; the Canadian wilderness
- Lovecraft's stories — foundational weird fiction; hypernaturalism; the Cthulhu mythos

### Music
- Brian Eno, *On Land* (1982) — ambient music as eerie landscape; acousmatic sound; Suffolk terrain

## Thinkers

- [[Mark Fisher]] — primary theorist of hauntology, the weird, and the eerie
- Freud — uncanny (*unheimlich*); Thanatos; negative hallucination; phylogenetic memory
- Lacan — jouissance; the Real; the speaking subject as gap
- Reza Negarestani — *Cyclonopedia*; inorganic demons; xenolithic artefacts
- Deleuze / Guattari — nonorganic life; becoming-animal (adjacent)
